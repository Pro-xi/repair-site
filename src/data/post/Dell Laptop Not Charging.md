## Why "Plugged In, Not Charging" is a Common Headache
If you're a Dell user in **Hutto** or **Round Rock**, you've likely seen the dreaded "AC Power Adapter type cannot be determined" message. Because Dell uses a specific "handshake" technology between the charger and the motherboard, a simple battery failure can often look like a charger issue—and vice versa.

## 1. Check the "Health" in the BIOS
Unlike other brands, Dell has a built-in diagnostic tool that is incredibly accurate. 
1. Shut down your laptop.
2. Turn it on and immediately start tapping the **F12 key** repeatedly.
3. Select **BIOS Setup** or **Diagnostics**.
4. Look for the **Battery Information** section.

If the health says "Fair" or "Poor," the battery cells are chemically depleted and need a physical replacement.

## 2. The Dreaded "Plugged In, Not Charging"
If your laptop runs fine while plugged in but the percentage never goes up, it could be:
* **The Center Pin:** Look inside your Dell charger plug. Is the tiny needle-like pin in the middle bent or missing? If so, the laptop won't recognize the charger’s wattage and will refuse to charge the battery as a safety precaution.
* **The DC Jack:** If the charging port feels loose or "wiggley," the connection to the motherboard might be frayed. 

## 3. Is Your Touchpad Lifting? (The Swelling Warning)
In the heat of a **Central Texas summer**, Dell Inspiron and Latitude batteries are prone to "swelling." If your touchpad is hard to click or the palm rest looks slightly curved, **stop using the laptop immediately.** 

A swollen battery is a pressurized fire hazard. At **Pro-Xi**, we safely extract these "spicy pillows" and recycle them according to environmental standards.

## 4. Why Genuine Parts Matter for Dell
Dell laptops are notoriously picky. "Universal" chargers from big-box stores often don't provide the correct signal to the motherboard, causing the laptop to throttle its speed to 10% of its capability. We always recommend and stock OEM-spec batteries to ensure your laptop runs at full speed.
