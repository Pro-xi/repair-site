# Liquid Damage 101: What to Do (and NOT Do) When You Spill Coffee on Your Keyboard

It happens in slow motion. You reach for your mug, a stumble occurs, and suddenly your morning coffee is soaking into your laptop keyboard. 

Panic sets in. You’ve heard the rumors about "putting it in rice," but before you run to the pantry, stop. At **Pro-Xi Tech Support**, we see dozens of liquid-damaged machines from Hutto and Taylor every year. Most of them are fixable—**if you follow these rules.**

---

### The First 60 Seconds: DO THIS IMMEDIATELY
1. **Force Shutdown:** Hold the power button down until the screen goes black. Do not wait for a "proper" shutdown. Seconds matter.
2. **Unplug Everything:** Remove the power cable, USB drives, and mouse. 
3. **The "Tent" Position:** Open the laptop and flip it upside down like a tent. This allows gravity to pull the liquid away from the motherboard and toward the keyboard.



---

### The "Rice Myth" (What NOT to Do)
**Do not put your laptop in a bag of rice.** Rice does not "pull" moisture out of sealed electronics. Instead, rice dust gets inside your ports, and the starch can actually create a sticky paste that traps the liquid against your sensitive components, accelerating corrosion.

---

### Why Liquid is a "Silent Killer"
The water isn't usually what kills a laptop—it's **corrosion**. 
* **The Science:** Liquid + Electricity + Metal = Rapid Oxidation. 
* **The Result:** Even if your laptop "works" the next day, the minerals left behind (sugar, milk, salts) will slowly eat away at the copper traces on your motherboard. A week later, it might never turn on again.
